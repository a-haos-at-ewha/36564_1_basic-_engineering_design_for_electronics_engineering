  3. Background


    1. Arduino: Arduino is an open-source integrated circuit (IC) development environment

      designed for learning and quick prototyping. Its main feature is the low bar for entry and the many tutorials and examples available to learn from.

    2. Android OS: The Android OS, created by Google, is an open-source operating system for hand-held devices. Somewhat less friendly for beginners than the Arduino, there are still many tutorials and examples available to work with.

    3. NFC: Near Field Communication (NFC) is a technology that allows data to be transmitted and received wirelessly from a un-powered card, token, pellet, or chip to a power delivering reader/transmitter. Subway payment card systems are the most common example of this technology but it has been used for identification, inventory-

      tracking, location-signaling, and many other systems.

    4. Electrical Relay: An electrical relay, in its most simple form, controls a large amount of current with a small amount of current.

    5. Amazon Echo: Amazon’s Echo device is a voice activate internet connected device that

      responds to voice commands and returns information based on those commands.

    6. Oscilloscope: Oscilloscopes are a common tool in an electrical engineers toolbox. Similar to a Digital Volt/Amp Meter (DVOM) the device creates a two dimensional representation of electrical signal with one axis showing voltage or current and the other axis showing time. This tool is critical for diagnosis and experimentation and based on previous works, it is assumed to be needed for the development of the NFC portion of the project. It is also assumed that once understood, it will lead to further understandings that could not be had without the technology.
